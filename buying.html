Buying a Chihuahua

Take your time, do your research, and you'll find the right dog for life. Here's everything you need to know.

Three Questions to Ask Yourself First

1. Am I ready for a 15–20 year commitment?

Chihuahuas are long-lived dogs. Before bringing one home, consider your life over the next two decades — future relationships, children, travel, and living arrangements. Who will care for your dog if your circumstances change? Make sure everyone in your household is genuinely on board.

2. Am I financially prepared?

A quality registered Chihuahua puppy from a reputable breeder costs $1,000–$1,500. That's just the start. Ongoing costs include:

  • Annual vaccinations (~$60/year)
  • Professional dental cleans ($300+ — Chihuahuas need these regularly)
  • Premium food to avoid digestive sensitivities
  • Vet checks and unexpected health costs

Pet insurance is strongly recommended. Be prepared for the lifetime cost of ownership, not just the purchase price.

3. Am I willing to do the work?

Chihuahuas need training and socialisation. A poorly socialised Chihuahua can become anxious and reactive — not because of the breed, but because of neglected development. Plan to attend puppy classes and commit to ongoing training.

🐾 The DCC can help you find a reputable breeder. Email us at dominionchiclub@gmail.com and we'll point you in the right direction.

Finding a Reputable Breeder

Don't buy from the first listing you find online. Most reputable breeders do not advertise on TradeMe or similar marketplaces — they have waiting lists. If you see multiple listings from the same seller or prices that seem too good to be true, proceed with extreme caution.

Look for Dogs NZ Accredited Breeders — they conduct health testing before breeding, limit litter numbers, and are accountable to the NZKC Code of Ethics. Even a pet-quality purebred Chihuahua should be registered with the NZKC.

Never buy from pet shops

Pet shop puppies are almost always sourced from puppy mills or mass-breeding operations. The puppies may appear healthy but often carry underlying health and behavioural issues that emerge later.

Red Flags to Watch For

🚩 Refuses to show you their home or the mother
🚩 Claims NZKC registration is "unnecessary"
🚩 Dismisses health testing or codes of ethics
🚩 Has many breeds or multiple litters available at once
🚩 Describes dogs as "loved like children" but keeps them in stacked cages
🚩 Won't discuss health history or DNA screening

Health Checks Before You Buy

  • Ask for proof of parental health testing (patellas, heart, SM where relevant)
  • For male puppies: confirm both testicles have descended — retained testicles carry cancer risk
  • Request DNA screening results where available
  • Ask about the breeder's vet and whether you can contact them
  • See the puppy with its mother and ideally its siblings

💚 Not ready for a puppy? Consider adopting a Chihuahua in need. The DCC supports NZ Chihuahua Rescue — a charitable trust dedicated to rehoming Chihuahuas across New Zealand.

Buyer's Checklist

  • ✅ NZKC registered breeder
  • ✅ Met the mother in person
  • ✅ Seen the breeder's home
  • ✅ Health testing documented
  • ✅ Both testicles descended (males)
  • ✅ Code of Ethics acknowledged
  • ✅ Puppy socialised & vet-checked
  • ✅ NZKC papers provided
